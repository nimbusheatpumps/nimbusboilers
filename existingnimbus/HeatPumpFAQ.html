Air Source Heat Pump Frequently Asked Questions

How do air source heat pumps work?
An air source heat pump draws heat from outside air using a refrigerant circuit, compresses it to raise the temperature, then delivers that heat to radiators or underfloor loops. For a full walkthrough see the Energy Saving Trust guide to air source heat pumps and the National Grid explainer.
How much do air source heat pumps cost to install in the UK?
Typical UK installation costs range between £7,000 and £13,000 according to the Energy Saving Trust, with recent surveys from Which? reporting similar totals. The GOV.UK Boiler Upgrade Scheme can cut upfront cost by £7,500 for eligible homes.
Do air source heat pumps work in cold UK weather?
Modern inverter heat pumps maintain output down to about −15°C by using defrost cycles and weather compensation, as outlined by the Heat Pump Association and the Energy Saving Trust.
How efficient are air source heat pumps compared to boilers?
Well designed systems deliver 3–4.5 kWh of heat from each kWh of electricity according to the Energy Saving Trust, while Which? lists modern gas boilers around 90% efficiency, showing the potential gain.
Do I need to replace or upgrade my radiators for a heat pump?
Many homes reuse existing radiators, yet upsizing key emitters or adding underfloor heating helps heat pumps run at lower flow temperatures. The Energy Saving Trust and Simple Energy Advice service both recommend reviewing emitters during the survey.
How long do air source heat pumps last and what maintenance is needed?
With regular servicing an air source heat pump typically lasts 15–20 years. The Energy Saving Trust maintenance guidance and the UK government Heat Pump Ready consumer guide outline filter cleaning, airflow checks and annual inspections.
Can air source heat pumps cool homes in summer?
Reversible models switch the refrigerant cycle to supply cooling through fan coils or underfloor loops. The National Grid explainer and the UK government Heat Pump Ready guide describe how and when to use the cooling mode.
Are air source heat pumps noisy?
Outdoor units usually run at 40–60 dB, similar to normal conversation, per the Energy Saving Trust. The Planning Portal confirms the 42 dB limit for permitted development in England.
Are we Gas Safe and MCS certified?
Nimbus Boilers & Heat Pumps appears on the official Gas Safe Register under ID 966812. Installations are delivered through an accredited MCS umbrella partner listed on MCS Certified so Boiler Upgrade Scheme rules are met.
What else should I know before getting a heat pump?
Focus on insulation, electrical capacity and an achievable install timetable so performance meets expectations. The Nesta homeowner guide and the Energy Saving Trust checklist cover the key questions to discuss.
What are the pros and cons of air source heat pumps?
Benefits include high efficiency, access to £7,500 Boiler Upgrade Scheme funding and lower emissions, while trade-offs include higher upfront cost and careful system design. Independent summaries from Which? and the Energy Saving Trust lay out the balance.
Is my home suitable for an air source heat pump?
Most homes with outdoor space for the unit and a wet heating system can host a heat pump once insulation is assessed. Suitability checklists from the Energy Saving Trust and the Simple Energy Advice calculator help confirm readiness.
Do I need planning permission for an air source heat pump?
England and Wales installs usually fall under permitted development when siting and noise rules are satisfied. The Planning Portal guidance and the UK government permitted development rules list the criteria.
How much can I save on energy bills with an air source heat pump?
Savings depend on the system you replace, the electricity tariff and the heat pump’s efficiency. The Energy Saving Trust shares scenarios versus electric, oil and LPG heating, while the UK government Heat Pump Ready guide explains when costs can undercut mains gas.
What is the Boiler Upgrade Scheme and am I eligible?
The Boiler Upgrade Scheme offers £7,500 grants in England and Wales when property, installer and heating replacement rules are met. Eligibility details are on GOV.UK, and Ofgem’s scheme overview sets out the application process.
How to size and design a heat pump system?
We carry out room-by-room heat loss calculations, check radiator outputs and set design flow temperatures to meet the MCS MIS 3005 standard. The Energy Saving Trust explains why this design work unlocks efficiency.
Can heat pumps provide hot water?
Yes, heat pumps feed dedicated cylinders with larger coils to deliver 55–60°C water efficiently. The Energy Saving Trust and Which? outline hot water considerations.
Are heat pumps expensive to run compared to gas?
With good insulation and smart controls, a heat pump can match or beat gas costs because of its high efficiency and access to flexible tariffs. The Energy Saving Trust compares running costs across fuels, and the UK government Heat Pump Ready guide explains when households see savings.
